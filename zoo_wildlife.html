Nestled in the heart of India, Jharkhand is a treasure trove of lush forests, rolling hills, and rich wildlife.

Explore Jharkhand’s untamed wilderness

Mahuadanr Wolf Sanctuary

Established to protect Indian wolves; part of the Palamau Tiger Reserve. Good if you’re interested in wolves and less-common predators.

Palkot wildlife Sanctuary

A less-visited area, decent birdlife, bears, wild boars etc. The habitat is somewhat fragmented but still nature-rich. It covers an area around 760 km2.

Palamau Tiger Reserve

Established to protect Indian wolves; part of the Palamau Tiger Reserve. Good if you’re interested in wolves and less-common predators.

Topchanchi Wildlife Sanctuary

Dry mixed deciduous forest, sal trees; you can see leopards (rare), deer, wild boar; also there is Topchanchi Lake nearby.

Betla National Park

It is Jharkhand’s only National Park. Home to tigers, elephants, panthers, sloth bear, sambhar, nilgai, etc.Its the only national park in the state.

Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary

Moderate wildlife, watchtowers, and water holes. Good option if you’re not traveling too far from Ranchi.

Udhwa Lake Bird Sanctuary

Best for bird-watching, especially migratory species. Very scenic, especially in dry/winter seasons. is India's first Ramsar-designated wetland .

Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary

Known for elephants, especially in summer when water is scarce elsewhere. Also offers forest walks, viewpoints in Dalma Hills.

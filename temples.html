Most Famous Tamples of Jharkhand

Baidyanath Dham

It is one of the most sacred Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva and is home to one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. The complex features the main Baba Baidyanath temple alongside 21 other smaller temples.

Jagannath Temple

The 100 feet tall temple was built by Thakur Aninath Shahdeo, in 1691 ADIt draws lakhs of devotees every year, especially on the occasion of car festival or Rath Yatra,

Sun Temple

The Sun Temple near Bundu, Jharkhand, is a modern religious site built on a small hill near Ranchi, designed to resemble a chariot with 18 wheels and seven horses, dedicated to Lord Surya

Chhinnamasta Temple

The Chhinnamasta Temple is popular for its Tantric style of architectural design. Apart from the main temple, there are ten temples of various deities such as the Surya, Hanuman and Shiva.

Maluti Temple

The Maluti Temple located in Dumka the cluster of 72 surviving terracotta temples, once part of a larger complex of 108. Built between the 17th and 19th centuries by the Baj Basanta dynasty.

Parasnath Temple

Parasnath is famous as Sammed Shikhar, the most sacred pilgrimage site for Jains, where 20 out of 24 Tirthankaras (Jain spiritual teachers) attained salvation on the Parasnath Hills in Jharkhand, India.

Dewri Temple

Dewri Temple, a 16th-century shrine dedicated to Goddess Durga, famous for its unique architecture with 16 idols of the Goddess and stone carvings. Landmark of tribal and traditional faiths of Jharkhand.

Harihar Dham

Harihar Dham is a Hindu temple in Bagodar, Jharkhand, notable for housing one of the world's largest Shivalingas, standing at 65 feet tall. This popular pilgrimage site, dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Naulakha Mandir

The Naulakha Mandir in Deoghar, is a beautiful and famous temple dedicated to Lord Radha and Krishna.It's known for its detailed traditional Indian architecture and carvings.

Bindudham Temple

Bindudham is a significant Hindu temple in Barharwa, Sahibganj district of Jharkhand, India, dedicated to the Tridevi (Maha Durga, Maha Lakshmi, Maha Saraswati) in the form of a Shaktipeeth.
